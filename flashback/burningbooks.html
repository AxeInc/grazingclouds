About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

The Burning Books

In the snowy mountains of the Balkan,
Crushed many in the race for Sarajevo,
And the siege of Bihac.

A steaming cup of tea for the kins
Was just a dream
In a land locked for survival,
As ethnic cleansing
Invited hell on earth.

Men were shackled not for any crime,
But for belonging to a different faith.

Women molested and raped for days
Delivered babies in thousands
By the folly of heinous lechery
Against a human race,
By those who floulted the Lord's Commandment,
Even, as Vatican longed
To enforce the Cannon Law.

To wash their blots and warm their flesh
Bosnian women saw nothing in their homes,
Except precious books, and
Bundles of paper left by their looters.

Their hearts were heavy,
As they moved the books In trollies to the burial ground
To burn them on a chilly night,
Knowing the cemeteries were safe,
Not only for the dead,
But for the doomed victims of militia;
Seeing their desecrated homes,
And the besieged camps
So bestial and horrid.

They sat around a country-fire,
Smiling and waving full of glow;
Throwing charred chests of superfine paper
Over fraternal tombs,
As they warmed
Their hearts in homage !
