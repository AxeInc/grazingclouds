About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

Up Above The Sky

A cold winter morning
Was a dull day
For all in Bombay.
The fury of a million dollar suit
Tore my mind,
While I raced
To catch my flight.

Shantilal shall file the vakkalath
Before the High Court of Bombay
To defend me in the vexing suit.

My aching hips did not dampen me,
As I climbed the Boeing in distress
Limping,each step, in deep pain.

In a state of profound sorrow,
I slipped into my window seat,
Feeling proud of my triumph.

II


While I closed my eyes
Before take-off; horrid thoughts
Flashed through my dreary mind.

Right from birth,
I was crippled
By my own blood;
Then on tranfusions
Prolonged my life.

The bleeding episodes, I had
Endured right as an infant,
Were battles for life,
Yet to be numbered.

A tooth extraction is a major surgery,
While a scratch anywhere is
Another blow, doctors will shudder.

Twinkling sensation in the stomach
Is the first indication of a fatal hemorrage
Deep in the belly.

Never had I felt the excitement,
As I grew from an infant
To inspid manhood,
After a barren adolescence.

III


As the take-off was announced
With music in the ear,
Depressive thoughts vanished like dew.

The climb was as smooth
As the flight of the little
Bird on the tarmac.

Then I saw from the window
An exciting panorama
I may never see again.

The Sun greeted the eastern sky
Warmly throwing vermilion
Of various hue in sheer delight

I know I shall never fail
To get justice from my Creator
For I have only faith in Him!

Those in the Bar and Bench,
Will verily come close to their
Conclusion, guided by Him alone.

For I have confidence
In my flight ahead
For my rightful place here, and hereafter.

IV


As long as my Sun beckons me
In appealing colors each morning,
The cold winter
Can only turn to warm summer.
The crimson rays will take me
From despair to hope, and
Tranquility beyond imagination.

I know then a bright sunny day
Will follow a chilly night;
Warming my sunken hearth forever.

I shall then face the world
With empathy and delight, while
I wade through life with greater sanity
