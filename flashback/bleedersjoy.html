A Bleeder's Joy

A born-bleeder*
Saw a trap,
While
Taking a turn
On returning home.

The crowd
Could never stop
The passing cars;
As fallen men
Lay in agony,
Like thingies
On the road
With broken heads.

The
Unwitting bleeder
Approached the scene
Full of blood,
In gay abandon.

How could he dare
To stop,
Like others before him;
Conscious
Of our murky laws.

Concern for the injured
Drenched in blood
Numbed his nerves,
Itching to help
Those lying
Almost dead.

The unknown victims
Were put in the rear,
One over the other,
Soaking the seat;
As a young lady
Got into the car,
Pleading to rush
Her kins
To the best surgery
In town.

Knowing
His poor legacy
On the the wheels,
He sped
Through the highway
So fast,
Blazing headlights
Before the cops;
Only to reach
The Trauma Centre
Miles away,
Within minutes.

Like a true Samaritan,
He leapt,
Calling for stretchers
For a mute examination.

They were rushed
For a prolonged surgery,
Only to be removed
For further care.

He came down the ramp,
Panting still;
As he prepared
To go home
After a day
Of utter triumph.

During the night
He dreamt;
He was bleeding to death
After a major crush,
While rushing through the streets
Without any care.

Moving to the hospital
Early in the morn,
He was told
How the victims
Had survived the crash,
Filling his heart
With so much Joy !!

*A Hemophiliac
