House Of MeHer

Seeing the lush greenery
In front,
I returned to MeHer
Long before dusk
To be pampered
In our heaven
On earth.

Nothing was amiss.
On a cloudy eve,
As I rested my sole
On the garden chair
In the patio,
With a pretty view,
Facing
The Alappat Cross Road
In the City of Cochin.

The Â€cool breeze
Was squeezing me,
As the coconut palms
Across the road
Swayed in fury
With the lashing
Of the whistling wind.

Slight drizzle
Made me puzzle
The nature Of the unusual rain.

With a sudden fury,
The mango trees In our neighbor's home
Were swinging in jerks,
Throwing so many fruits
On the road
That was so wide.

In drenching rain
Many ran for cover,
Pushing
Their little ones
Along the way.

In front of me
Across the palms,
A passenger train
Was yelling;
As it moved
With caution
To enter
Ernakulam Junction.

The rains continued,
Spraying
So much water,
Drenching me
All over;
As Meher ran
For a towel to dry
My dripping soul,
Knowing well,
I enjoyed The thunder and the shower,
Like the kids on the road.

The cars were wading
In knee-deep water,
As a fiery lightning Struck like an inferno;
Forcing my wife
To run inside,
Pulling me along
To the safety of MeHer.

Then we heard
The shrill siren
From the Shipyard
On the western front,
Nodding the end
Of another day.

Inside MeHer, it was cosy
As ever, as the children
Cuddled besides the TV
Gazing the stars,
Before the lights drooped;
Throwing us all
In utter darkness,
Till we snored
In the dead of night;
As it showered
Without a break
All through the night.
