About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

The State Of Kerala

The messy traffic jam
Opened my eyes,
Seeing the glut of those
Working for our welfare.

The entire chain of command
Was there,
As the state cars battled
To uphold the greatness
Of their stoic masters.

As a poor soul,
Licking many a wound,
I saw the public servants
Limping ahead, displaying
Their name plates in red,
Proclaiming their status
In the State of Kerala.

As I walked briskly,
It gave me
So much thrill,
When the wailing sirens
Could push
The cars by inches;
Forewarning me,
Not to get hooked
To the icons of power,
While I die to seek justice
In front of creeping authority.

Even to fight for my rights,
I must seduce my master
With my charm and money,
Spending lavishly
My time and effort.

Like a river, the benefit gushes
From the Himalayas
To the barren field down below.
From the Chief
To the debased men in the hierarchy,
They are one, like a human chain.

Even without a whistling carriage,
You accumulate enough baggage
To be charmed by so many,
By having only a broken chair
To call your own;
And a dusty bundle of files in your care
To fleece the rich and poor alike;
Always feigning you are asleep;
Knowing well, in a democracy
Who is the king.

I prostrate before you
To grease your base,
Knowing my life
Depends on your grace;
For you think
I could be ejected
From the frying pan
Into the fire.

May god bless you
More than me.

Parties may go;
The rulers may come,
We are never our masters;
This I learnt long ago.
