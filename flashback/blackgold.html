About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

The Vanishing Black Gold

Drilling deep wells,
Spilling the black oil,
Ready to be moved
Through pipelines,
Stretching

For miles and miles
In the refinery
Cracking produce
Half a barrel of gasoline
From every barrel of crude.

All is not well,
For each drop
Goes up as vapour,
Never to be re-used,
As we dig on,
Knowing fully well
Nature's bounty
Will never last
Beyond a century.

Meanwhile,
A tyrant ordered
Troops to march
On their bellies,
Ordering them
To walk over
Their neighbour's chest,
Declaring
He shall fight
The Mother
Of All Battles,
Only
To be humiliated
By the powers
Across the seas.
As he retreated,
He burned
Many a well
Full of oil,
Causing
Trillion dollar damage
To a tiny kingdom.

The black gold
Will soon leave
Mere rocks,
With nothing more
To offer
Than ravaged
Dwellings
For the future.

Let mankind
Search and research
For an alternate source
Of energy,
Efficient to meet
Future demands,
Causing no pollution
Whatsoever.

We need
To exchange the petro-dollar
For an unknown super-energy
To be perfected soon
By our hallowed scientists.

Should a holocaust
Bring even the Sun
Too close to our doors,
Like the eternal flames
Of the oil refineries,
Spitting soot
And chemical waste
On the dot,
As we approach
These stinking monsters
With awe
In utter disgust.

On a cloudy night,
As we saw
The lighted towers,
I wondered
Why so many stars
Were coming down
With a warrant
To blast
These temples
Of Modern India
To kill and maim
So many!

Only we can
Thank our stars,
If we are alive
To enjoy
A chilly night
On
A rainy day !!
