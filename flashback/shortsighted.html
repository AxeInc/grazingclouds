About Mr. A. Khader

Strictly Short-Sighted

In the pitch dark sky,
Never we saw twinkling stars,
As the power failed,
Plunging all in patent darkness.

Sighting a mighty star
Over a coconut palm,
I shouted: "My dear,
See the blazing star,
Full of fire work;
A kaleidoscope;
Sitting pretty
Over the coconut cluster".

"Don't be moony
On a gloomy night,
Lest, I must carry you
In the morn to the Doctor
To mend your mind",
So said My Fair Lady.

After she uttered
Her prophetic words,
Electric waves lighted
Every lamp I could see.
Then I shouted full of joy:
"I see more flowery stars
Before me".

My Dear Lady
Rushed me inside
In bitter haste,
Muttering
Prayers all the way.

Next day,
Before noon,
Umpteen tests
Probed my mind,
Only to find
I was sane.

The doctors said
With all their might
Not to think
Of an asylum,
Till passions cooled.

Soon, a genius thought
My eyes be probed
For failing sight.

A sane advice indeed:
For, with glasses
The abberations vanished,
And I stopped seeing stars
Over the palms,
And on the plains.

After many days,
My beloved giggled
With a beaming smile;
Saying,
What I saw was
Indeed the full-moon
Over the coconut-palms
Before our home,
Where we had anchored
In peace
On a moonlit night.
