About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

A Will To Save

The cryptic warning
Made him rush
From his house, Just before the storm;
As a pot

Of flowery plant With a stench of death
Crashed to the floor.

A fleeting glance
Showed him Miles of kneel,
Coiled With hairy roots.

The fallen plant
Was bleeding for love,
As he left it
To the wonder care
Of his charming wife.

Rushing straight
To the Trauma Centre,
He was aghast,
Seeing mangled bodies
Of the bomb blast victims;
Many with exposed veins
And dangling arteries.

Tiny roots he saw
Before leaving home
Was warning enough
Of what was in store;
As drenched bodies
With sheets of blood
Shooting from the hearts
Lay bare before a crowd.
He rushed to the Chief,
Knowing well
The anatony lessons:
Our heart is unique,
Showing the nobility
Of our Lord;
Least understood
By our evil genius.

Men forget
The ever murmuring
Flesh wailing wailing for love,
As they poison
Other's hearts
For their own needs.

Blinking;
He knew
The entire net-work
Circulating blood
Measured thousands of miles,
Much more
Than the profile
Of Mother Earth.

To save man
Or his nerve,
Was never so easy;
As putting fallen plant
Into another crate
With little more of earth,
And sprinkle of water,
With a doze of manure
To restore the damage
Unseen
By the naked eye.

That was life, as it stared
Before him on the afternoon
Of the Black Friday,
As he went on slicing
Limb after limb;
Never counting the number,
Even for a second.
