The Dawn, So Dark

I saw the dawning hope,
As my son left by train
To Kollam town,
Within minutes of arrival
On a crowded platform.

He had a shoulder bag,
Full of books,
And a bundle of clothes.

He left with a smile,
Saying, he would
Follow the rules.

We heard the clatter
Of the gleaming express,
Blazing forward
Whistling stirring tunes.

Entering the car,
My pious lady
Bowed;
Seeing the weather
So bad !

Being so numb,
It flashed to her
The darkest hour Was
before the dawn !!

Great hopes we had
From our progeny;
Remembering the battles
He fought,
Right from the start.

Slowly we did move
To city streets
On the chilly morn
To find more gloom
Under the overcast sky.

The dawn at Cochin
Soon shall usher
The Sun, flashing
Beacons of hope
For all,
While they moved
From their homes
To face a rainy day,
Full of lightning
And thunder !

In rain or shine
We heard the chime,
As we counted the dimes
.
