Man Of Letters

Mani Iyer was born
With a silver spoon
In his mouth,
Only to find him
Declared a pauper.

In the thatched hut
He owned
As an encroacher,
He had a collection
Of a million words.

Only last year,
Lakshmi
Left him forever,
Leaving him
Reams of paper
They collected daily,
Sifting dust-bins,
While moving around
Their peaceful abode.

Brooding over her death,
He never felt sad
Over the legacy
She left in sorrow.

With no issues,
He knew
His lasting companion
Was the printed word
Thrown away
By many with scorn
In the streets
Of the City State.

Kach morning,
As Mani entered
His heavenly abode
With fresh collections
Of Golden letters,
He was proud
To be a connoisseur
Of the heady wine
That gave always
A kick
To stimulate
His frontal lobe.

After his death,
He had willed
His archives
To the urchins
He loved to teach,
Spending not a dime:
