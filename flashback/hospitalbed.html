From The Hospital Bed

From my bed
Inside
The towering Hospital,
I saw
The winding traffic;
Down below
The city streets.

Beneath,
Life was
Fast as ever,
While
I was bleeding,
More
Than I could
Swallow,
Seeing only red,
For so many days.

As the morning mist
Played hide and seek;
I gazed
Far away, looking
At the "floating"
Sports Centre,
Conceived by
One, who
Always called me "Boss"
Hardly ever
Pleasing me
Even By flounder.

I hated His sneer,
As he only
Mocked
Knowing My calling.

Nearby,
I saw the temple
That is never
Twisted,
Though, the locals
Called it
"Valanjambalam"

Just in front,
A tourist home
Was busy
As ever.

Not far away
I could hardly see
The publishing giant
With whom
I had
Many ties;
Before we parted,
Throwing my pen
In utter disgust.

From my gums
Blackish gore
Gushed out,
Full of clots, till
I closed my lips;
While turning pale
With the stench
Of blood.

As I dozed
In the ICU,
The Doctor,
Who won
My trust
Patted me
With a grin
Relax, and
Drive home;
