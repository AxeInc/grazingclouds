About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

Don't Sympathise, Please Empathise

Mute handicaps, and
Crippling disorders
You endure
Can never bother you,
So long as you
Look deep in the eye
Of every storm.

With your body in pain,
Your mind can never
Race beyond your walls.

None can ever
Raise your spirits,
While you dream
The whole world
Is against you.

You know well
God is standing by
Ever close to you,
Holding-out,
So much solace.

The Doctor of Medicine
May be sure,
Why your blood Is full of clots.

He goes on counselling,
With a lot more empathy;
That is his only tool.

The only one,
Who can cure
Has no time To hear your woes,
For your time is up,
Before you open
Your mouth.

In-born
Disorders can annoy
Your body,
And shatter your mind.

You may face
Lot more trauma
After umpteen tests;
Making your body
A veritable mine,
Which no doctor
Will ever touch,
Even with
A cotton bud.

That is life for you,
And for me,
While we live
With care,
Moving our body,
And spirit;
Here on earth.
No man
Is ever born equal,
Yet we fight To excel.
Some have all the luck;
As we crawl on this earth,
While others
Make funny gestures
At those below par.
To be born ill
Is never a crime
For you to sneer,
When I creep And glide;
As you fly-past,
Looking down
On a blooming soul,
Like me,
Who has never been
In your way.
