About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

A tribute...

This site is an ovation to the late poet from his children and grand children, who love him ever-so dearly. We hope to keep his legacy alive by setting up this website, containing every last poem from his book of poems, Grazing Clouds.

A Flashback

An interactive introduction to the life of M.A Abdul Khader.

This section of Mr.Khader's mesmerizing collection of poems delivers an insight into his glorious past. From his appoitment with a dentist to the serenity he is pampered by at home, this section provides a realistic intuition of his life. "God's Own Country" is a sad half-satire, it beats with love of the beautiful land the late poet was born in. "Et tu Doctor" is central to the collection, it brings out the turns and knots that make the poet. We can only sympathise with him when he says that he went to the doctor and "parked his junk" before him; in the end he says, the doctor probed his tummy but not his heart. Read on for pleasure!
