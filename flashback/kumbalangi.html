Kumbalangi

Well before dusk,
From Perumpadappu
The ferry set sail,
As breeze swept
Over serene waters,
Beneath the haze of heat.

Many were dressed to kill,
Looking pleased
In the scorching sun.

The warm backwaters
Made the island of Kumbalangi
Endearing to our hearts,
As the country boats towed us
To the eastern gateway.

Greeting us straight
Was a tavern,
Offering a local brew,
Stronger than toddy,
Forbidden by many a guru.

As it drizzled,
The Maruthi
Slipped through the streets
Of yet another paradise;
A nature's bounty
Unrobed by man.

Much to our surprise,
In pouring rain
A few tonsured aliens
Were passing by; chanting-
"Hare Krishna, Hare Rama;
Hare Krishna, Hare Rama……”

The apparel the locals wore
Distinguished their tribes;
While sectarian thoughts
Were drowned in the sea.

In the oasis of peace
Ladies never wore sarees
Or the traditional attire;
For they were more at ease
In their nighties,
Even during day, copying
Their affluent sisters,
Who spend their days
In their house coats,
Almost like a night gown,
Merely by habit.
The nightie phenomenon
Was a craze everywhere,
Amongst the young, and
The not-so-young
In the main-land,
Flooding the island
With a fashion so faddish.

Oh; East follows the West
West follows the East,
And they shall ever meet !!
