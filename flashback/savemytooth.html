Save My Tooth, Doctor!

Marina was not the girl I loved,
She was the best beach in town.

As a student, I had run
Around, gazing
At the statue of Labour,
Reminding me, I must
Never struggle hard.

Every student
At the Institute
Ought to be medically fit,
So insisted the college rules.

How I got inside is no mystery,
As the doctor found
Nothing strange
On mute examination;
Least he knew,
I was crippled
By my own blood.

The hassle of life
At the Institute
Made me important
For a while.

Life was indeed serious
For all,
As they fought
Only to excel.

For me, minor bruise
Caused so much bleeding
And painful trauma.

Heavy medications
Never eased the situation.
Having lost some teeth
I pleaded with the doctor,
"Please save my tooth"

I could neither
Open nor close my mouth,
Least I could munch
My favorite pie.
Liquid diet
Was the only answer.

Soft drinks
Gave me deep pain,
And warm beverages compounded
The torture.

I was between the devil
And the root-cancel scare.

The thought of my going
For dentures
Early in life,
Rattled my nerves,
Pounding my heart.

I knew then, my tooth
Was a million dollar tool,
Grinding every meal.

Whatever I may earn
On leaving the academy,
Can only be stashed
On a mountain top,
As I suffer and groan,
While I graze
Here and there.

God is so real
While your tooth
Is in trouble,
When you have
None for solace
Except Him !

A single tooth in decay
Remind us
How blessed we are,
As we kneel Before our Lord !!
