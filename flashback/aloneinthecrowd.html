Alone In The Crowd

Move, Move,
The conductor ordered;
While I climbed the bus,
As the passengers
Pushed and heckled
Before a busy street.

Without a seat
In the teeming carriage,
I glanced at the crowd;
Seeing none whom I knew.

After a while,
Sitting by the window,
I saw a colorful view
Of pedestrians
On the footpath
In the city centre,
Where I had stayed
For so many years.

I knew,
My worst enemy
Was trailing me;
As a full blast
Of exhaust fumes
Numbed by throat,
Blinding my eyes.

I found no friends,
As I moved around
The Queen of the Arabian Sea,
Till I landed
Near the crowded jetty,
Where I saw
Ever so many,
Rushing from the boat.

I found
Neither a friend nor a foe;
Except the molesting fuel
Throttling me,
During my ride.

I was ashamed
To be a stranger
In Kochi
As I returned
To my abode
Dying for breath.
