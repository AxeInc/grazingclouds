About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

Believe Not Your Eyes

In the land of coconut palms
I travelled from dawn to dusk.

The sultry afternoon soon passed,
Ushering the cool evening breeze.
On our way to Cochin, once proclaimed
The Queen of the Arabian sea.

The twilight hours saw me
Swooning in the rear,
Throwing my spectacle without care,
Enjoying moments of rest I craved.

Moments passed, as quickly
As I slept, only to open
My eyes as we came
To a sudden halt, before
A speeding coach on a highway.

In front of me I saw a filigree
So beautiful, crafty and artistic
That I kept on staring for long
Through my naked eyes with joy.

I saw flowery patterns aglow,
While looking at carriages plying
Below the yellow street lamps,
And near the houses around me.

The decorative designs I saw
Were twinkling stars
That have fallen before me,
Moving all around, emitting
Celestial patterns everywhere.

While I closed, and opened my eyes
I found them to be real,
Before my senses could disprove
What I saw was a dazzle.

Before long I dozed, dreaming
They were the modern
Horse brasses, well polished
To reflect light and distract
The evil eyes of those on the roads.

How could my mind, least, accept
These horse brasses tagged to the fast
Moving carriages on the street,
As I should abide by better reason.

Breaking my induced sleep,
I wore my goody specs,
Only to find these bright patterns
Were the shining lights, showing us
The unending path to safety
On a dark summer night.

These were neither falling stars,
Glittering diamonds, kaleidoscopes,
Nor bright horse brasses, I had imagined.
They were the faddish electric lamps,
Blazing us through total eclipse
As we raced in the dead of night.

I knew, my passions went wild
With so much confusion for a while,
As I saw the scenes before me,
Forgetting I should never believe my eyes !
