About Mr. A. Khader

The Legacy

There he sat,
Waiting for his dentures,
Charming me
With his toothless grin

As he tarried his turn,
He spouted a manly tale.

Under the British,he toiled hard;
Before the worst crush
Fell on his top.

He was often warned,
As he climbed the Ghats:
Hope for the best, and
Be prepared for the worst!

The head injury sunk the forehead
Beyond the scalp, portending his nemesis

At the Brooke Bond; the union he had
Was serene like the mountain brook.

He was soon flown to London town,
Only to return,
Bouncing back full of joy,

Years passed, as he enjoyed life;
To retire in style,
In the Venice of the East.

He received his pension
On the dot,
Showing with pride
A recent cheque in utter gleam.

More than the four-figure dole,
Gratitude was etched
In every paper the company sent.

That made the legacy
Of the Raj truely great!

Still a bachelor,
He detests the hassle;
As he pause and wait
For his daily munch.
