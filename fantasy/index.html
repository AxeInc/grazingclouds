About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

A tribute...

This site is an ovation to the late poet from his children and grand children, who love him ever-so dearly. We hope to keep his legacy alive by setting up this website, containing every last poem from his book of poems, Grazing Clouds.

The Fantasy

An overview of the imaginative parrelel universe the human race craves so much.

This partition of the book, Grazing Clouds, potrays the poet's imaginative side. Though the poems in this section don't completely comply with the title, It is a fusion of fantasy and verisimilitude. Click the title or image to view poems from this section. "Et tu Doctor" is central to the collection, it brings out the turns and knots that make the poet. We can only sympathise with him when he says that he went to the doctor and "parked his junk" before him; in the end he says, the doctor probed his tummy but not his heart. Read on for pleasure!
