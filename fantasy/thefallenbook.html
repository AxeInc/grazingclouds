About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

The Fallen Book

The front door of the mansion
Lay wide open with a hefty book
Put as a. doorstop, as I wondered
How long the massive text
Was used in the new role
Outside the book-shelf;
Finding more care,
Besides the costly doormat.

Years back, in an Arab land
I went searching for my passport,
As the Immigration Chief
Said he could never find it, forever.

Out of rage he shouted:
Bring a new one from your stupid embassy,
Otherwise , I could never leave
The country to witness
The last rites of my beloved,
Far away in my homeland.

In desperation, I begged to the Chief,
If I could search before him.
Seeing my arrogance,
He shouted simply: You fool,
Don’t waste my time !

At last,
I could enter his office,
While policemen trotting sten-guns
Stood by, in front.

Scanning the bundles
Of documents

Of many nationalities,
I could never find mine.

Then, looking at the floor,
Where the Chief sat,
I saw a solitary passport,
Snuggling and supporting
His uneven chair.

With awe and fear,
I showed the boss
The unique wedge,
As he bent his bulky mass
With a bitter heart.

As he got up with a limp,
I jumped at the book,
Only to find
It was mine.

Soon, the Police Chief
Took out the seal,
Blighting my fate By stamping my exit
In desparate hurry,
For my final departure.

In utter joy I shouted:
Sukran,
Sukran Jessilan.*


*“Thank you very much” in Arabic.
