About Mr. A. Khader

His Excellency

The god almighty
Was whistling past,
In bullet-proof cars,
With royal comforts.

The dark window-panes
Gave him only tainted views
Of the suffering humanity,
As the people stared
While the Minister relaxed
In heavenly gilt.

The arrival
Of his Excellency,
With a row of
Black Cats
In Gypsies.

He had total security
From the people,
Whom he loved to rule,
Though, he was
In the eye of a storm.

Someone threw a rocket
Banging his car
With a mighty thud
Sending shock waves;
Ordering
For a red alert.

The driver
In utter confusion
Ran for cover
To a nearby shop,
Leaving his master
To his own fate.

The Black Cats
Went into action,
Leaving many a dead.
While the situation Eased,
They found
His Majesty,
With his breed Inside;
Panting for breath.

None came to his rescue
On that Deevali night,
As he was driven non-stop
To the All India Institute
With many more outriders;
Only to be declared :
Dead on arrival.

The Nation
Mourned his death
Caused
By a flinging cracker
In utter freedom
By the urchins,
As they yearned
To relax,
Besides the shanties,
Out-side the city streets
Of the Capital,
Better known to many
As the great
Indraprastha.

A pall of gloom
Turned the festival of light
Into a chilly night.
