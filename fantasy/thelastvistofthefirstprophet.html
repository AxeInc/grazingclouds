About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

A tribute...

This site is an ovation to the late poet from his children and grand children, who love him ever-so dearly. We hope to keep his legacy alive by setting up this website, containing every last poem from his book of poems, Grazing Clouds.

The Last Vist Of the First Prophet

Adam,
The father of man,
Was seen greeting His family,
Unfazed
In his XL-Cosmos,
Fully equipped to meet
Any eventuality
From the planet
He loved to breed
The human race.

XL-Cosmos is much more
Than the Air Force One.
It could travel Around heaven and earth,
With speed much.
Beyond light,
With comforts,
Befitting those in heaven.

The Air Force One
Will fly away
Into thin air
By the powerful blasts
From the XL-Cosmos,
Jamming
All its electronic blitz,
Throwing
The powerful incumbent
Down the Hudson river.

Adam loves to travel
With lightning speed,
To see us
On our planet,
Like a father,
Iching to see
The welfare
Of generations
In human bondage
He conceived
In the garden of Eden.
Then, he knew
The temptation
Of Eve
Was beyond
The control
Of even the prophet
Within him.

He could not mend
His sole believer, then;
Knowing fully well
His mission to reform
His offspring
Would require millions
Of prophets like him
For centuries to come.

None ever questions
His paternity,
Nor his materity

Can we ever Imagine
Our real Papa
Is circling
Eternally within us
Just for our welfare.

For all of us
He is real,
As we carry Streams of blood,
And the flesh
From the living cells
Of Adam and Eve.

While motherhood
Conceived us
, God Almighty watched
In utter delight !

The XL-Cosmos
Is unique,
For it can shrink
Into a ball-point
Or expand like a globe.

The view
In front of the pilot,
Who triggered
The human race
Gave moving pictures
Of men and matters
With heady sound
Like a TV hook-up,
Showing man
In many dimensions,
Giving
The very aura
Of homo sapiens.
The multi-colour
Mini-screen
Offers so much more

The laser beams carrying
Clear visuals
In three dimensions
Probing deep
Into our body,
Baring
Our inner mind
And our very soul
With ease,
Like a doctor
Scanning the brain
For a malignant growth.

Grand-old Papa
Could divine all these
And more,
Only to find
Ways and mean
To hug millions
He cares
To love.

During his last visit,
In deep torment
He saw A cute baby,
Just born
In a dingy shelter,
With bullets
Sticking out
From her gory eyes;
Crying in anguish
Besides her dying mother.
Scanning beams
Brought the firework
In sharp focus,
