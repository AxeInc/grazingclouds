About Mr. A. Khader

In A Restaurant

He is the odd man
Out, alone
In a city restaurant.

His tainted face
And the limbs showed
Many a sore,
Inviting wrath
More than pity.

His attire
And his gait
Revealed his fortune.
None cared to mind
His enticing stare,
As he was an eyesore
To all, while he munched
His delicacies
With so much delight.

Soon he turned
Towards the guests,
Sitting far and near.

A young couple
With an infant sat dumb,
As their hearts
Went for a dive,
Bringing memories
Of many a date
In blossoming company.

So sad he felt
Seeing the gloomy couple,
Young and beautiful;
Still warm in his memory.

By the table on the right
Were gluttons
From a Family of Five,
Tasting each other’s choice
Of the spicy South Indian cuisine,
Throwing all manners
Under the table.

He went on gazing
Their fun and frolic,
Until his skin turned red,
Out of passion and fury;
Knowing he could never
Dream to enjoy so such gusto
Ever in his life.

Staring
At his pigmented limbs,
He went on probing
Why romance ceased
To be an issue
For the charming couple,
After the birth
Of their bonny baby ! !
