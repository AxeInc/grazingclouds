Mr.& Mrs.Lowe

As the setting sun
Cast long shadows
In the cold winter evening,
We were approaching
The hill station of Nainital.

While we climbed the steep roads,
We saw love-birds, all the way.

The romance we nurtured
Had elements of surprise.
As we came down the valley,
She stopped hugging me.

Pleasantly, she put all her weight
On my back, rubbing and
Tickling me by her sensuous touch,
As she rested on the pillion
Teasing and taunting me.

I was itching to relax,
Along with my sweet-heart,
In the warmth of a hotel room,
Up above the mountains.

The Bullet carried us
Through the serpentine route
To the hill station
Over the snowy peaks of the Himalayas.

Our love was passionate, yet, tender.
We were quite smitten,
Like the lovers we saw
All over the land.

There was no reason
To blink before the counter,
While crossing the last hurdle
To share the pleasures
In the cosy room by the hearth,
As we were burning
With passion,
During the midnight hour.

The message in the hotel
Registers were straight and clear.
We were Mr. & Mrs. Lowe,
For the rest of the world,
While anchored in five-star luxury.

As we entered the room, my sweetie
Chuckled with an infectious smile-
“Are we husband and wife, dear ?
Though, cohabitation is a thrill,
Is it not a sin, Lowe darling” ?
“Nope” came the prompt reply.

After stimulating drinks,
Followed by a steaming meal,
The mood was exciting
And, while the love-birds slept,
Mrs. & Mr. Loy/e plunged
Into a wild midnight affair.

The jazzy music and the smutty movie
On the telly took their senses
To dizzy heights before the climax,
While drawing the curtain for the night;
After praying for fresh fantasies
To turn into reality, before long.

“Promiscuity is very much In the blood of so many
That only a miracle can
Transform the gene within you”-
Said the vice sage Of Kamattipura, long ago !
So reminded the noble Lowe
Before they slept
On the chilly night.

The Holy Book Was lying all night
Uncared, offering nothing exciting
Except the Lord's Commandments.
