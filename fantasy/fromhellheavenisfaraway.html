About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

From Hell,Heaven Is Far Away

In the Balkan mountains
I was running
Behind flocks of sheeps,
In front of my sweetheart,
Who never understood
My real faith.

She was a charming girl,
Who ever swore by the cross,
Saying Jesus died for our sins.
For many years
We lived
As best of friends,
While I prayed
Five times a day
In my home,
Up above Sarajevo;
As we found
A fatal attraction
Brought us too close
To unite us
In marital bliss.

Years passed,
As we enjoyed our life
In this creamy world,
As neighbours praised
Our heaven on earth;
While
We nourished our faith
Deep in our heart.

One day, my rustic daughter
Shot at me point blank,
Asking why I did not
Wear a cross like mummy.

I said:
We are all men of God,
And follow the sermons of Abraham,
Whether we call ourselves Muslims,
Christians, Jews,
Or by any other name.

It does not matter
What you are,
So long as you are a Man,
Practising a faith
Dear to you,
Without harming any soul
Or destroying
What is God's Creation.

I told her -
Faiths may differ
But, we must
Pray to our Lord.

As years rolled fast,
Many things happened
In our mountain town,
When the followers of Abraham
Became the worst of enemies;
Spitting fire,
Not only from the throat;
But from the mountain top,
And the thundering sky
All over Bosnia.

Days looked like years,
As men brutally killed
And cut each other With inborn spite;
Molesting and raping,
Thousands of young,
And not-so-young women
In the melting pot,
For no fault of theirs,
Just because we belonged
To ethnic groups.

As the gospels
Churned in the hearts
Full of melody;
I heard a parady,
Never heard before:
None can love you
Better than thee;
And bury thy neighbour
Without a wink,
Fot the grater glory
Of our creed !

The other day,
I was in a shanty
And I could get
Nothing to carry home
To my starving family,
Reeling without water
And gas for many months.

Then,grenades
Full of splinters
Were lobbed at us;
Killing hundreds.
And wounding many
In a savage war
On an unarmed crowd
In the centre
Of our hilly town.

There was no ambulance,
No police and
No rescue for the victims
For hours.

In a crude hospital,
Stone age surgery
Made me groan
In utter pain,
As they sliced
My hands
And the lower limbs,
Till I fainted;
Only to open my eyes
Hours after the torture.

Without even surgical wool,
I was rotting,
When my family came;
Only to be told
By those in the clinic
To carry me home,
To make room for others,
Coming like a swarm.

Back home,
In our barren house,
My only comfort
Was a craggy cot
Pushed close to the window,
Without even a shutter.

I saw the meadow
Below the valley,
Reminding me
Of our teenage romance;
While she went on
Counting her rosary,
As the cross
On her necklance
Went on dangling.

From my death-bed,
I saw
The smouldering pine-trees,
And the blue sky,
Cheering My inner soul.

A whiff of breeze
Suddenly cooled
My aching body,
Reminding me
That God was very much
Alive;
As the leaves over the trees
Moved in ecstasy,
With every breath of air
From our Lord;
Perched far away,
Watching the finale
Of yet another life,
Here on earth.
Maggie thundered in anger
In my dying ears:
Dear, you will soon
Go away from us
To your heavenly abode !

She gave me her last kiss,
Before my eternal sleep;
Little before the Angles came
To snap my soul
From my battered body,
That was waiting
For an unknown grave
In the land I loved
To live and die.

As I closed my eyes,
Did I hear
The wailing prayers
Of my beloved ones,
As they went on murmuring....
Ameen, Ameen, Ameen ;
While a raging crossfire
From land and air
Left may country
Bereft of life,
Like
A hell on earth.
