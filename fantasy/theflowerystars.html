About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

The Flowery Stars

On the highway,
I saw the glare
Of incoming traffic,
And the red lights
Of a caravan
Moving in front.

Full of strain
I wiped the eyes,
Throwing my glasses
In utter relief.

After a while
My naked eyes saw
Shimmering patterns
Of various hue
As carriages sped past
Before me.

A thousand flowery stars
Passed through my inner mind,
As I gazed at naked lights
On the streets.

The rear lights Of cabs in front
With reddish rays
Of shining stars
Made me wonder
How Christmas can fall
On a sultry night
In New York State !

While I began dreaming,
The highway patrol
Came rushing,
As I had steered
To a wrong track;
Almost blind
Without my specs.

With haste,
I grabbed my glasses,
Wearing them with jitters,
Only to find the stars,
And the flowery signs
Suddenly vanish,
Without a warning;
As I pushed me
Firmly to my seat.

Only, then did I realize
The warning of my doctor:
Never drive Without the glasses
Even for a while;
Lest, you see illusions
Of heaven on earth !
