About Mr. A. Khader

Poetry had come a mite late to Mr. Khader, though involved in all that went on around him,he had yet a detachment which is a product of his invisible eye, a joy of life, an awareness of eternity, an acceptance that life is a piece of glass that could shatter at the slightest shock.

For Progress We Demolish

To build is so laborious,
And demolishing
Is indeed a hassle.
Fleeting thoughts
Criss-crossed my mind,
Seeing broken homes
On either sides
Of the winding freeway.

People love to make
Their dwellings
Closest to the roads;
Only to be thrown-out,
Before they warm their hearths;
As the arterial highway
Has to be widened,
Seeing the ever-growing
Flow of unending traffic.

An old lady stood
Over a peg mark,
Telling us all-
She is on the road;
Waiting for a handsome purse,
Promised by her benign masters,
For her tiny plot,
Over which an array of caravans
Shall ply before long.

Her wealthy neighbour
Sunk his dinars
Into a mansion;
Breaking it, now brick by brick;
Leaving it mangled
Like a concrete monster.

Though eviction is so painful,
It's a pleasure to be acquired
With a golden tag.

The century old house,
Proud monument
Of an ancient family
Was in shamble;
Razed to the ground
With jutting logs
And planks of teak
Piled like a pyramid.

A shrine,
Which had a thousand
Lamps on holy days
Was without a spark
On a windy night;
As a temple
Coming up in the rear,
Had stirred the holy spirit
.
Chapels and minars
Tumble like a pack of cards,
As the freeways
Long to carry many more souls,
Bustling with renewed zeal !
