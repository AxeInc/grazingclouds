About Mr. A. Khader

The PM At Dawn

The morning flight
Was getting ready,
While the crew at Palam
Wait before dawn.

At the abode of the PM,
The household is agog,
As he woke-up early
To drive far away,
Without his clones
In a northerly route
Under tight security.

Meanwhile,
A PM stepped down
His palatial bungalow,
To be greeted royally
By the orderlies
And his canine brigade.

Minutes passed by,
As he raced through
The streets of Delhi,
With no cops To guide
His limousine.

The signals everywhere
Were flickering amber,
For he had no reason to doubt
The fate of his mission.

He must reach the airport Soon,
without any hamper,
As a PM he had so much to do.

A cat-nap brought him
Before Indira Gandhi,
Where he saw gun-trotting
Policemen everywhere
With walkie-talkie,
Busy with their morning core.

As a well-known PM,
He came tumbling down.
There was neither
A ceremonial send-off
Nor a 21-gun salute,
As he climbed
The Boeing waiting for him.

Gracefully, he was ushered in
By those charming ladies,
Leading him to the seat,
Specially chosen for him.

Soon he realized,
He was not the PM,
Though he was indeed
A Project Manager Of a growing company,
Turned multi-national
With the weight Of the might PM,
And his petty cronies.

As a proud PM,
He was sitting pretty In the forefront
Of a Jumbo
Used by PMs alike,
While they travel
In the best of comforts,
Without the dazzle
Of the Supreme Leader.

Verily, the PM is a potential hazard
To himself and his companions,
Oozing little glamour and glitter,
As he race
Through the problem states.
